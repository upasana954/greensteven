What is Video Streaming, and how dedicated servers are used for streaming business

It’s commonly used for live events, such as concerts or speeches, but can also be used for on-demand content, such as movies and TV shows. While video streaming has been around for a while, it’s only recently that businesses have started to use it as a way to reach their audiences. And with good reason! Video streaming offers a number of advantages over traditional methods of communication, such as television and radio. Not only is video streaming more engaging and interactive, but it’s also more flexible and cost-effective. What is a Video Streaming Server

A video streaming server is a type of server that is specifically designed to stream video content to viewers. Video streaming servers are typically used by businesses that need to deliver video content to a large audience, such as online video providers, news organizations, and educational institutions.
 
 Dedicated servers are often used for video streaming because they can provide the high-bandwidth connections that are necessary for delivering quality video content. Additionally, dedicated servers can be customized to meet the specific needs of a business's video streaming infrastructure.
